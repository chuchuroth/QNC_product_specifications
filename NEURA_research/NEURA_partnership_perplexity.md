## Confirmed relationships

**Bosch** is the clearest strategic partner beyond the names you already had. NEURA and Bosch announced a technology and development partnership in January 2026 to scale humanoid robotics and Physical AI from Germany, with joint collection of real-world work/motion/environment data in Bosch facilities and co-development of AI core software and interfaces. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/neura-robotics_we-are-joining-forces-with-bosch-to-advance-activity-7417124084121198592-t2gZ)
That makes Bosch more than a customer or brand alliance: it looks like a data-rich industrial training and deployment partnership that can materially improve NEURA’s models and product readiness. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/neura-robotics_we-are-joining-forces-with-bosch-to-advance-activity-7417124084121198592-t2gZ)

**Qualcomm Technologies** is the clearest semiconductor and platform collaborator. NEURA and Qualcomm announced a strategic collaboration in March 2026 focused on next-generation robotics and Physical AI, combining Qualcomm’s Dragonwing robotics platforms with NEURA’s embodied AI stack. [roboticsandautomationnews](https://roboticsandautomationnews.com/2026/03/15/neura-robotics-and-qualcomm-enter-strategic-collaboration-to-advance-physical-ai-and-cognitive-robotics/99677/)
The public messaging emphasizes edge execution, low latency, reliability, and a “brain + nervous system” architecture, which suggests Qualcomm may become a core compute/reference-platform partner rather than just a component supplier. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nakulduggal_ew26-dragonwing-activity-7436842325869334528-yxzi)

**Delta Electronics** appears as a significant investor and ecosystem partner. NEURA’s January 2025 Series B included Delta Electronics, and later posts from NEURA-linked and Delta-linked accounts describe collaboration on AI-powered robotics for smart factories, integration with Delta’s DIATwin digital twin platform, and access to NEURA’s Neuraverse. [bioregio-stern](https://www.bioregio-stern.de/en/news/neura-robotics-secures-eu120-million-in-series-b-funding-to-propel-cognitive-and-humanoid)
This is important because Delta brings manufacturing reach, factory automation context, and potential deployment pathways, not just capital. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/arnenordmann_welcome-to-the-neuraverse-excited-activity-7298314279814877185-Jeh0)

**Drees & Sommer** is a newer strategic partner focused on “robotics-ready buildings.” Their February 2026 announcement says they will co-design buildings and infrastructure that interact with humans and robots in real time, using sensorized environments, digital twins, and IT/OT networks. [dreso](https://www.dreso.com/de/en/news/details/neura-robotics-und-drees-sommer-starten-strategische-partnerschaft-fuer-robotik-ready-gebaeude)
Strategically, this is useful if NEURA wants robots deployed into instrumented facilities where the environment itself becomes part of the product stack. [neura-robotics](https://neura-robotics.com/neura-robotics-and-drees-sommer-partnership/)

**ek robotics** is not a conventional supplier relationship; it looks like ecosystem expansion through acquisition or absorption. In October 2025, NEURA’s CEO described ek robotics as joining “our family,” with global customer base and mobile robotics expertise being integrated into the Neuraverse. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dregerofficial_neura-ek-robotics-a-new-era-of-mobile-activity-7379428149312323584-eyR7)
That suggests NEURA is building an end-to-end industrial robotics channel, not only a humanoid platform. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dregerofficial_neura-ek-robotics-a-new-era-of-mobile-activity-7379428149312323584-eyR7)

**Zimmer Group** remains a confirmed strategic partnership, and it fits NEURA’s end-effector / flexible automation layer. [neura-robotics](https://neura-robotics.com/neura-robotics-and-zimmer-systems/)
Because Zimmer is well known in industrial gripping and automation, this likely matters for application engineering and deployment more than for core AI. [neura-robotics](https://neura-robotics.com/neura-robotics-and-zimmer-systems/)

That is a useful early sign that NEURA is not only chasing humanoids, but also sector-specific automation in construction and building-related workflows. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/neura-robotics_sto-buildingwithconscience-neurarobotics-activity-7054790423197700096-TPuF)

## Strategic outlook

The most likely next alliances for NEURA are in three areas: semiconductors and edge AI, industrial data partners, and system integrators/deployment channels. [neura-robotics](https://neura-robotics.com/neura-robotics-and-drees-sommer-partnership/)
If NEURA continues its current pattern, expect more relationships with facility operators, factory automation firms, digital twin / OT network specialists, and robotics hardware ecosystem players that can feed data into Neuraverse or help scale installations.
